Discipline

The cost problem isn't visibility. It's timing.

01
The invoice is already too late.
Commitments compound before invoices arrive. Architecture choices, vendor agreements, reservation logic — none of these wait for the finance team. By the time a report surfaces the number, the decision behind it is already old.
02
Cost is a design input, not a report.
The teams getting this right aren't doing more analysis. They built cost into the decision itself — before the architecture review, the vendor commitment, the roadmap sign-off. The report comes later. The decision doesn't.
03
Finance and engineering need one language.
When engineering and finance share the same cost model, decisions happen in one meeting instead of three. No translation layer. No waiting for the next sprint review to find out what something actually cost.
What changes
Cost gets modeled early, before commitments start to compound.
Utilization becomes a core operating metric, not an afterthought.
Architecture decisions carry explicit economic tradeoffs.
Finance and engineering work from a shared cost language instead of translating after the fact.
